Technology Disruptors Competition — where educators showcase groundbreaking uses of technology in the classroom for a chance to win a $1,000 cash prize. Open to all teachers each fall, this competition invites educators to push boundaries, innovate, and make an impact. Tech My School hosts, supports, and highlights their efforts while serving as partial judges in this exciting process.

Tech My School presenta con orgullo la Competencia Technology Disruptors — donde los educadores exhiben usos innovadores de la tecnología en el aula por la oportunidad de ganar un premio de $1,000. Abierta a todos los maestros cada otoño, esta competencia invita a los educadores a romper límites, innovar y generar impacto. Tech My School apoya y destaca sus esfuerzos mientras actúa como juez parcial en este emocionante proceso.

Tech Trailblazers (everyday tech for learning), Digital Creators (student expression through technology), Data Wizards (data-driven instruction), Young Entrepreneurs (business & entrepreneurship), and AI Wizards (AI-powered classroom experiences). Guided by Darlene Colón, Director of the Technology Disruptors Program, teachers receive support throughout the year to develop transformative projects. At the conference, attendees can explore all 10 finalists' projects during coffee breaks, with displays in Salon 4. Three expert judges — one from the TMS team and two guest speakers — select winners in each category. Awards ceremony: Saturday April 11th at 4pm!

Los maestros compiten en cinco categorías: Tech Trailblazers (tecnología cotidiana para el aprendizaje), Digital Creators (expresión estudiantil a través de la tecnología), Data Wizards (instrucción basada en datos), Young Entrepreneurs (negocios y emprendimiento), y AI Wizards (experiencias de aula con IA). Con la guía de Darlene Colón, directora del Programa Technology Disruptors, los maestros reciben apoyo durante el año para desarrollar proyectos transformadores. En la conferencia, los asistentes pueden explorar los 10 proyectos finalistas durante los recesos, con exhibiciones en el Salón 4. Tres jueces expertos — uno del equipo TMS y dos ponentes invitados — seleccionan ganadores en cada categoría. Ceremonia de premiación: ¡Sábado 11 de abril a las 4pm!

🎯 2026 Finalists

10 educator-led projects selected from 14 submissions — showcasing innovative use of technology in Puerto Rico classrooms

1
🤖 Narrabots
Maestra Ariana Colón
Robertson's Private School · Fajardo, PR
Students designed AI-assisted robots, created stories with ChatGPT, and built digital books with Book Creator. Reading data collected via Seesaw to personalize instruction.
3rd GradeEspañolFajardo
2
🎭 Voices of History: A Living Museum Experience
Maestra Wilmarys Valentín
Robertson's Private School · Fajardo, PR
A student-created virtual museum using ChatGPT, HeyGen talking-avatar videos, and Spatial.io — transforming historical figures into immersive digital experiences.
9th–11th GradeHistoriaFajardo
3
🍽️ Taste4All
Maestro Bryan Rivera Medina
Esc. Sup. Francisco Morales Rivera · Naranjito, PR
A lab simulation for students with Autism using VR, tablets, and data logging. Students develop innovative recipes — including for NASA's Astronaut Culinary Challenge in zero gravity.
SEM AutismoCienciasNaranjito
4
🗺️ Learning Roadmaps: A Tech-Powered System for Reflection, Voice & Mastery
Maestra Marina Osorio
Christian Day School · San Juan, PR
A data-driven personalized learning system using ClassDojo, Khan Academy, Wayground, and Book Creator to track progress, support instruction, and build student metacognition.
3rd–6th GradeMatemáticasSan Juan
5
💻 Learning with Code Combat
Maestro Alexander Santana
Paradiso College Preparatory · San Juan, PR
Python learning turned into an epic adventure. Using Macs and Google Classroom, students access CodeCombat — going from zero to creating complex solutions with full autonomy.
7th–8th GradeTecnologíaSan Juan
6
🧭 Building Maps, Revisiting Routes, Appreciating Our Legacy
Maestra Valerie Martínez
Paradiso College Preparatory · Arecibo, PR
Students used NASA WorldView satellite imagery and National Geographic MapMaker to trace the routes of the Caribbean's first settlers, producing a data-rich analytical map.
8th GradeEstudios SocialesArecibo
7
🎵 Bunny Ears & Superheroes: Teaching Through Songs
Maestra Mariana Bermúdez
East Point Academy · Las Piedras, PR
6th graders used Suno to create songs, then taught 2nd graders how to tie their shoes — combining peer mentorship with AI-generated music. "Bunny ears" can be heard all day in the hallways.
2nd–6th GradeTecnología & MúsicaLas Piedras
8
🐺 Wolves the Unmuted
Rosana Rosario
A school video podcast where students run a real production studio — scripting, editing, and operating pro equipment like OBS Studio. Students master digital media, social media strategy, and public communication while building emotional intelligence and teamwork. The school mascot becomes their voice to the world.
Student Voice
9
🎮 Game Defenders: Programadores Contra el Ciberacoso
Maestra Norma Muñoz Varela
Esc. SU Francisco Felicié Martínez · Vega Alta, PR
5th graders learn Python programming via CodeSpark while simultaneously learning to identify and combat cyberbullying — transforming classroom culture and building empathy through tech.
5th GradeTecnología EducativaVega Alta
10
🤝 When AI Meets Empathy: How AI Turned Wonder into a Learning Experience
Maestra Alejandra Rentas
UNIB Escuela Laboratorio CeDIn · San Juan, PR
AI chatbots scaffolded deep literary analysis of Wonder by R.J. Palacio. Groups of 2–3 students built character analyses with academic, emotional, and organizational AI support.
5th GradeInglésSan Juan
